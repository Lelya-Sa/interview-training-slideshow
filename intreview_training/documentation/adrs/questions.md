# ADRs (Architecture Decision Records) - Interview Questions

## Questions (1-8)

### 1. What is an ADR?
**Answer:** Architecture Decision Record. Document capturing important architectural decisions, context, consequences, status.

### 2. Why create ADRs?
**Answer:** Document decisions, communicate rationale, historical record, onboarding, knowledge sharing, prevent repeating discussions.

### 3. What should an ADR contain?
**Answer:** Title, Status, Context, Decision, Consequences, Alternatives considered, Date, Authors.

### 4. When should you create an ADR?
**Answer:** Significant architectural decisions, technology choices, design patterns, trade-offs, decisions affecting multiple teams.

### 5. How do you format an ADR?
**Answer:** Markdown format, numbered (ADR-001), consistent structure, clear and concise, version controlled.

### 6. How do you maintain ADRs?
**Answer:** Keep in repository, review regularly, update status, deprecate outdated decisions, link related ADRs.

### 7. What is the difference between ADR and design document?
**Answer:** ADR: focused on decision, context, consequences. Design doc: detailed design, implementation, broader scope.

### 8. How do ADRs help teams?
**Answer:** Understand why decisions made, avoid repeating discussions, onboard new members, maintain consistency, learn from past.
